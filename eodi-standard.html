EODI Standard Overview

This page is a practical index to the standard. Each domain includes a short “why it matters” preview, then links to the full domain guidance.

Minimum bar, not optional extras

A credible EO data platform should be modular, API-first, secure, and resilient, with consistent data contracts, strong governance, and event-driven delivery. If a domain is weak, operations slow down and trust erodes.

1. Infrastructure

Why it matters: unified infrastructure removes data silos and manual handoffs so tasking-to-delivery works as one dependable flow.

Read full domain

2. Security

Why it matters: without strong access control, encryption, and auditability, mission-critical users cannot trust the platform.

Read full domain

3. Automation

Why it matters: automation reduces latency and human error, making EO workflows repeatable and integrable at operational tempo.

Read full domain

4. Consistency

Why it matters: normalized formats and metadata let teams analyze immediately instead of constantly repairing data contracts.

Read full domain

5. Governance

Why it matters: policy enforcement, licensing controls, and audit trails are required for lawful use and enterprise adoption.

Read full domain

6. Reliability

Why it matters: time-sensitive missions fail when platforms fail; high availability and tested recovery are non-negotiable.

Read full domain

7. Scalability

Why it matters: EO data and user demand grow continuously, so the platform must stay fast under much larger workloads.

Read full domain

8. Notifications

Why it matters: event-driven notifications cut response time and let downstream systems act automatically instead of polling.

Read full domain
