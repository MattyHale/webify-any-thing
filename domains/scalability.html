Scalability and Performance

Scalability allows capability to persist as demand increases.

What is Expected

The platform must scale across data volume, users, and compute without degrading performance. Elastic infrastructure, efficient indexing, caching, and distributed processing are required to support petabyte-scale archives and high concurrency.

Why It’s Important

If performance collapses under growth, adoption stalls. Efficient search and delivery keep analysis fast and costs predictable.

Key Scalability Principles

Elastic Resource Scaling

Autoscale compute, storage, and queues based on demand.

Optimized Indexing and Retrieval

Use spatial and temporal indexing and cloud-optimized formats for fast queries.

Parallel and Distributed Processing

Partition workloads across workers or clusters for linear scaling.

Continuous Performance Tuning

Benchmark and optimize hot paths regularly.

Geographic Distribution

Use regional replicas and CDNs to reduce latency.
