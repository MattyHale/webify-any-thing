Reliability and Resilience

Reliability determines whether the platform works when conditions are least forgiving.

What is Expected

The platform must deliver consistent performance with minimal downtime and no single points of failure. Resilience requires redundancy, geographic distribution, monitoring, and tested disaster recovery plans.

Why It’s Important

Many users rely on EO data in time-sensitive operations. Outages and data loss erode trust and force costly workarounds. A resilient platform stays available and communicates clearly during incidents.

Key Reliability Principles

High-Availability Architecture

Use redundancy, multi-AZ deployments, rolling updates, and zero-downtime changes.

Data Redundancy and Backups

Replicate data, verify backups, and meet defined recovery objectives.

Resilient Processing and Delivery

Retry transient failures, checkpoint long jobs, and absorb spikes with elastic capacity.

Monitoring and Alerting

Track health, performance, errors, and service-level objectives with proactive alerting.

Regular Testing and Drills

Run load tests and failover exercises routinely.
