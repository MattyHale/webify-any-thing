Governance and Compliance

Governance keeps growth controlled so scale does not become disorder.

What is Expected

Governance ensures data is used lawfully and in accordance with organizational and contractual obligations. Access rules must be clearly defined and enforced in code. Data lifecycle controls such as retention, deletion, and provenance tracking must be built in.

The platform must provide auditing and reporting so administrators can answer who accessed what data, when, and under which conditions.

Why It’s Important

Many customers have strict compliance requirements. Without strong governance controls, adoption may be impossible. Governance protects providers and users and enables confident, compliant use of EO data.

Key Governance Principles

Access Governance and Permissions

Model roles, groups, and dataset permissions with approval workflows, revocable sharing, and audit trails.

License Management and Usage Compliance

Encode license rules and enforce geographic, temporal, and redistribution constraints automatically.

Auditing and Accountability

Maintain tamper-evident logs and provide tools for review and reporting.

Data Lifecycle and Retention

Apply retention, archival, and deletion automatically and verify backups.

Legal and Regulatory Alignment

Honor residency, export controls, and sector policies through technical enforcement.
